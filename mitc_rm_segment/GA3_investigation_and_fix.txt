================================================================================
THIN TAPERED-SQUARE GA3 (C33) DEFICIT : INVESTIGATION AND FIX (independent omega_3)
================================================================================
Section: mitc_rm_segment/ .  Operator under study: segment_element_general.py
(GENERAL RM taper shell -> Timoshenko 6x6).  Solid reference: FEniCS 3-D solid,
examples/data/benchmark/taper_square_solid_{iso,m45}.npz.  Env C:\conda_envs\opensg_2_0_env.
Harness: dbg_ga3.py .  Fix: segment_indep.py (+ driver run_indep.py).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. SYMPTOM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Tapered SQUARE tube (flat walls, mid-surface mesh, t/R=0.02 thin, aR=0.7), single ply.
Beam transverse-shear stiffness GA3 (Timoshenko C33) under-predicts vs the 3-D solid:
  thin iso  C33 -24.4% ,  thin m45 C33 -39.9%  (C36 = GA3-EI2 coupling tracks it, -39.7%).
GA2 (C22) is fine (-1% / +5.5%).  Thick wall (t/R=0.2) is fine (-5.7% / -1.3%).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. DIAGNOSIS (each step is a dbg_ga3.py experiment; all reproduced)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 - Localised to the tapered SEGMENT: prismatic boundary RINGS are clean & symmetric
   (thin_m45 ring C33 +0.9% vs segment -39.9%).
 - NOT discretisation / NOT quad distortion: axial refinement NL=10..80 leaves C33
   flat (-24.8% / -40.2%) even as trapezoids -> parallelograms.  Converged operator error.
 - 2nd-order in taper, thin-only: extra deficit ~ (dR/dz)^2 ; ~0 for thick walls.
 - NOT the transverse-shear STRAIN rows (BGe/BGh/BGl): ablating all their taper terms
   moves C33 <1%.  (Overturns the code's own comment on lines ~423-427.)
 - The transverse shear is generated THROUGH the omega_3-drilling-in-curvature (Lambda)
   mechanism: LAMBDA_ON=0 collapses BOTH GA2 and GA3 to zero in the segment.
 - Denominator C33 = n.b3 = 0 identically on the two flat walls that carry the GA3 shear
   flow (normal || b2).  BUT: GA3 is insensitive to the C33 regularisation --
   C33_EPS (shear Tikhonov) and FLOOR33 (curvature floor) sweeps over 100x change C33 by
   <0.6%.  So the singular value is NOT the quantitative driver.
 - FRAME-ROTATION test (rotate the cross frame so no wall has C33=0): at 45deg every wall
   has C33=+-0.707 (healthy, no floor) yet GA3 is STILL -28.7% (and GA2 degrades to match).
   The shear trace GA2+GA3 is NOT frame-invariant (2.20 -> 1.80 x1e9) -> the operator is
   NOT frame-objective on flat walls.  The axis-aligned frame is a LUCKY special case that
   rescues GA2 (its walls align, cosines exactly 0/1); GA3's walls sit at C33=0.
 - Square-specific: the CIRCLE tube (C33=0 only at isolated points, walls curved) is clean
   and symmetric (C33 +3.7% / +4.9%).  Flat walls (k22=0) + taper is the root.

ROOT CAUSE: the RM drilling omega_3 is eliminated algebraically via
  omega_3 = S/(2 C33) - (C3b/C33) omega_b ,  C33 = n.b3 ,
which on flat walls (C33=0) is degenerate; the eliminated-then-regularised drilling is a
frame-non-objective, ~-24..-40% mis-representation of the flat-wall tapered transverse shear.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. WHAT DID NOT WORK (tested, negative)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 - Neglecting the flat-wall drilling (DRILL_TOL: zero Lambda+shear 1/C33 on |C33|<tol):
   collapses GA2 AND GA3 to zero -- the drilling there is load-bearing.
 - Removing the singularity from the denominator (better/consistent 1/C33): cannot help --
   GA3 is provably insensitive to the 1/C33 value (floor sweep).  The singularity-free
   rewrite of the SAME strain (via the finite drilling angle phi_3) needs 2nd SG-derivatives
   of w, which C0-RM forbids; so with omega_3 dependent + C0 the 1/C33 is unavoidable and
   its conditioning does not set GA3.
 - Frame rotation (square): backfires -- 45deg makes GA2=GA3=-28.7% (two orthogonal flat
   wall-sets; rotating helps one, hurts the other).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THE FIX : omega_3 as an INDEPENDENT 6th DOF  (segment_indep.py)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Keep the pre-elimination kinematics: omega_3 is a genuine nodal DOF [w1,w2,w3,om1,om2,om3]
and appears DIRECTLY -- no 1/C33 anywhere:
   curvature   Lambda_a = omega_3|a + x_{1;a} omega_3'     (raw field derivatives)
   shear       2g13 += C23 om3 = x_{3;2} om3 ;  2g23 += -C13 om3 = -x_{3;1} om3
   membrane    unchanged (no omega_3)
The in-plane symmetry that DEFINED omega_3 is re-imposed in its FINITE (undivided) product
form as a penalty on the drilling residual
   DR = C33 om3 + C3b om_b - S/2   (= C33 (om3 - om3_eliminated); finite even at C33=0),
   S/2 = 1/2[ k1(x11 Rn2 - x12 Rn1) + w'_i(x11 x_{i;2}-x12 x_{i;1}) + (w_{i|1}x_{i;2}-w_{i|2}x_{i;1}) ].
On healthy walls (C33~1) the penalty pins om3 to its eliminated value (recovering the
general operator); where C33=0 the residual drops om3 and om3 is set by its own curvature
stiffness -- no singularity, no null mode.  Boundary: reuse the validated 5-DOF rings for
dofs [0..4]; om3 (dof 5) is left free at the boundary (natural BC).

PENALTY (D-scaled).  pen = pen_beta * max|diag(D)|  (default pen_beta=0.1).  Rationale: a
penalty must be commensurate with the elastic stiffness it competes against -- large enough
to enforce DR=0, small enough to avoid ill-conditioning.  An ABSOLUTE pen only lands in the
good window for one material/thickness; scaling to D makes pen_beta dimensionless and
portable.  Plateau: pen in ~[1e5,1e9] absolute (iso thin) gives a stable answer; pen>=1e11
breaks from conditioning.  pen_beta=0.1 sits safely inside for all cases tested.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5b. STRONG IMPOSITION VARIANT : LAGRANGE MULTIPLIER (no penalty, no pen_beta)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
run_indep.shell_solve_lagrange + segment_indep.assemble_constraint.  One nodal multiplier
per node enforces the WEAK drilling constraint <N_a . DR> = 0 EXACTLY via the augmented KKT
   [ Dhh  G^T ][ w ]   [ -Dhe ]
   [ G    0   ][ lam] = [ -Ge  ]  (+ the w' coupling Gl in the l-block),
G/Gl/Ge = nodal-weak DRh/DRl/DRe.  Multipliers append to the DOF vector; the existing MSG
V0/V1 condensation runs unchanged on the augmented matrices.  No penalty, no conditioning window.
Agreement with the penalty confirms the fix is real (not a penalty artifact):
   thin sq iso  GA3  penalty -3.1% | Lagrange -4.0%   (GA2 -6.0/-6.3, GJ -2.6/-7.5)
   thin sq m45  GA3  penalty -2.4% | Lagrange -4.3%   (GA2 -12.0/-11.5, GJ -4.5/-9.0)
Lagrange GJ is slightly softer; GA2/GJ residuals (field-om3 discretisation + free-om3 end BC)
are the same in both. Lagrange is the cleaner production choice (no pen_beta tuning).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. AIRFOIL (BAR-URC blade) NOTE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On a real airfoil the GA3 shear flow is carried by the CURVED skin (circle-like), not the
flat web.  Independent omega_3 barely changes the blade seg-5 m45 GA3 (tap-vs-ring-avg
+14.1% -> +13.7%), i.e. the blade's tapered-vs-average deviation is most likely REAL taper
compliance, not the flat-wall formulation error -- confirmable only with a blade 3-D solid.
