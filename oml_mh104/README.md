# oml_mh104 — mh104 OML-reference Timoshenko results

Self-contained OML-reference deliverable for the **mh104** composite airfoil: Timoshenko 6×6 beam
stiffness vs wall-thickness factor `f`, comparing three shell models — **JAX-Kirchhoff (C1-Hermite),
JAX-Reissner-Mindlin (C0), and FEniCS-shell** — at the **OML reference (frac=0)** against the
**VABS-validated FEniCS 2D-solid**. Order `[EA, GA2, GA3, GJ, EI2, EI3]`; reference point =
quarter-chord. All 21 nonzero 6×6 terms are plotted, one panel each, with a companion %error set.

Generated by `../mh104_thickness_study/` (CCW contour traversal, k22=0 flat facets). This folder
holds only the OML plots + data; the center/IML references stay in the parent study for later work.

## Cases
`f ∈ {0.1, 0.2, 0.4, 0.6}` — every lamina thickness scaled by `f` (outer contour fixed).

## Headline results (OML vs solid)
- **JAX-Kirchhoff and JAX-RM bracket the solid on the shear terms:** Kirchhoff *over*-predicts GA2/GA3
  (+7→+30%, +17→+75% over f=0.1–0.6), RM *under*-predicts them (GA2 ≈ −40%, GA3 −26→−10%). The solid
  sits between.
- **EA, GJ, EI2, EI3 agree well for the JAX models** (≤6% at thin walls); GJ within ±5% across `f`.
  GA3's residual (~+17% even when thin) is the Kirchhoff transverse-shear model limit (refinement
  plateaus, not a bug); RM's transverse shear is softer.
- **FEniCS-shell is the odd one out at thin walls:** its flapwise GA3/EI2 **collapse for f=0.1–0.2**
  (GA3 −100%, EI2 −85% at f=0.1) and EA/GA2 are off (−17/−26%); it recovers by f=0.4–0.6. This is the
  C0+penalty shell struggling with the degree-3 web junctions / thin-wall conditioning, where the JAX
  C1-Hermite is robust. For in-plane GA2 at f≥0.2 FEniCS-shell actually tracks the solid best.
