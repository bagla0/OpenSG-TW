WHAT IS BEING COMPUTED
----------------------
The wall of a thin-walled beam is modelled as a laminated plate.  Its constitutive
law relates the 8 plate stress resultants to the 8 plate strains:

    [ N11 N22 N12 | M11 M22 M12 | Q1 Q2 ]^T  =  K_8x8 [ e11 e22 2e12 | k11 k22 2k12 | 2g13 2g23 ]^T

with the RM plate stiffness in block form

              | A   B   0 |          A = membrane (3x3)
    K_8x8  =  | B^T D   0 |          D = bending  (3x3)     B = membrane-bending coupling
              | 0   0   G |          G = transverse shear (2x2)

The 6x6 classical block [[A,B],[B,D]] is the Kirchhoff-Love ABD.  The 2x2 G is the
RM addition.  BOTH are obtained by MSG, i.e. by solving a one-dimensional
"structure gene" (SG) through the wall thickness -- NOT by classical lamination
theory algebra and NOT with a 5/6 shear-correction factor.

MSG lineage: the plate SG is the through-thickness line; the plate law is the
condensed 3-D energy of that line under the plate macro strains.  This is the
plate specialisation of Yu's Mechanics of Structure Genome; the RM construction
(the extra G, no correction factor) follows Yu (2005), "Mathematical construction
of a Reissner-Mindlin plate theory for composite laminates", IJSS 42:6680-6699,
and the shell generalisation Yu, Hodges & Volovoi (2002), CMAME 191:5087-5109.
[NOTE: the exact route this code uses for G is the complementary-energy shear-flow
method (Whitney 1973 lineage); see PART 2 for how that relates to Yu 2005.]

VARIATIONAL STATEMENT (docstring lines 118-122)
    The 3-D strain at a through-thickness point is split into a MACRO part driven
    by the 6 plate strains and a FLUCTUATION (warping) part v(x) (x = through-
    thickness coordinate):

        Gamma_3D(x) = Ge(x) . eps_plate  +  gamma_h(v)

    Voigt order (laminate frame):  Gamma = [e11, e22, e33, 2e23, 2e13, 2e12].

    Ge(x) = the MACRO strain-concentration (membrane + x*curvature):
        Ge = [[1,0,0, x,0,0],       e11 = eps11 + x kappa11
              [0,1,0, 0,x,0],       e22 = eps22 + x kappa22
              [0,0,0, 0,0,0],       e33 = 0   (from macro; the fluctuation supplies it)
              [0,0,0, 0,0,0],       2e23 = 0  (from macro)
              [0,0,0, 0,0,0],       2e13 = 0  (from macro)
              [0,0,1, 0,0,x]]       2e12 = 2eps12 + x*2kappa12
      -> code lines 208-211 build exactly this Ge each Gauss point.

    gamma_h(v) = [0, 0, dv3/dx, dv2/dx, dv1/dx, 0]  (docstring line 119)
      the through-thickness gradient of the 3 warping displacements v=(v1,v2,v3);
      it feeds ONLY the transverse rows e33, 2e23, 2e13 -- i.e. the warping fixes
      the transverse response (transverse normal + transverse shear) that the plate
      macro cannot represent, exactly the physics a plane-stress CLT drops.
      -> B = _plate_B(nodes_xi, xi, he) at line 206 is this gamma_h in FE form.

FE DISCRETISATION (lines 152-186)
    - layer_bot[k]  : running ply interface positions bottom(OML)->top(IML).
    - C_layers[k] = rotated_stiffness_6x6(E,G,nu,angle) : the FULL 3-D anisotropic
      ply stiffness (6x6) in the laminate frame -- NOT the reduced plane-stress Qbar.
      This is essential: keeping the full C is what lets the SG recover e33 and the
      transverse response instead of assuming plane stress.
    - quadratic (3-node) Lagrange elements, 1 element/ply (exact for uniform plies),
      3+ point Gauss (line 186).
    - node_x = node_x - z_ref (line 184): the reference surface is placed by SHIFTING
      THE MESH ORIGIN.  z_ref = 0 -> OML (bottom), sum(thick)/2 -> mid/centroid,
      sum(thick) -> IML.  This is the mesh-based reference change the FEniCS OpenSG
      plate solver also uses; it is equivalent to a parallel-axis shift of the
      assembled ABD (see shift_abd_reference) and leaves G unchanged.

ASSEMBLY (loop lines 193-219)
    Per element e, per Gauss point q, with dw = 0.5*he*w_g the physical weight:
        K     += B^T C B  dw            (lines 213)   -> fluctuation stiffness (ndofs x ndofs)
        F_load+= B^T C Ge dw            (line  214)   -> coupling load (ndofs x 6)
        D_ee  += Ge^T C Ge dw           (line  215)   -> uncondensed macro energy (6x6)
    Mass moments mu, mu_x, i22_m (lines 217-219) for the mass matrix (not used here).

NULLSPACE + SOLVE (lines 221-238)
    The 3 rigid-body translations (v1=const, v2=const, v3=const) are the kernel of K.
    They are removed by PROJECTION P = I - Q Q^T (Q = orthonormal basis of the 3
    translations), NOT a penalty:  a penalty alpha*Q Q^T leaks alpha onto every node
    and swamps a near-zero ply (gelcoat E~10 Pa) -> singular.  Projection keeps soft
    plies well-scaled.
        K_proj = P K P + beta Q Q^T            (line 235)
        V0     = P . solve(K_proj, -(P F_load))(lines 237-238)  -> warping (ndofs x 6)
    V0 is the through-thickness fluctuation produced by each of the 6 unit plate strains.

CONDENSED PLATE STIFFNESS (line 240)
        D_eff = D_ee + V0^T F_load
    This is the MSG condensed energy.  Because V0 = -K^{-1} F_load (on the projected
    space), V0^T F_load = -F_load^T K^{-1} F_load < 0, so
        D_eff = D_ee - F_load^T K^{-1} F_load          (Schur complement)
    i.e. the warping REDUCES the naive D_ee to the true plate stiffness -- the
    standard MSG/VAM condensation.  For n_per_layer=1 and uniform plies this is exact.

RETURN (lines 244-254)
    stiff = D_eff (6x6).  With shear_refined=True it becomes plate_8x8(D_eff, G) (PART 3).
    With return_warping=True it also returns warp = {V0, node_x, elem_layer, C_layers,
    elem_order, angles} -- the object the DEHOMOGENIZATION reuses (see dehom doc PART B).

 PART 2 -- THE 2x2 TRANSVERSE-SHEAR BLOCK G (the RM addition)
           transverse_shear_stiffness(thick, angles, mat_names, mat_db)

WHERE THE FORMULAS COME FROM (docstring lines 8-19, physics)
    The transverse shear stiffness of a laminate CANNOT be gotten by summing the ply
    G through the thickness with a universal 5/6 factor -- that is layup-blind and
    wrong for sandwiches (a soft core in series with stiff skins).  Instead the code
    uses the classical equilibrium/complementary-energy construction:

    (i) PURE BENDING in-plane stress (per direction a; x=ply-1/beam, y=ply-2/tangent):
            sigma_11(z) = Qbar_11(z) (z - z_na) kappa
        with the neutral axis z_na chosen so the net axial force vanishes:
            z_na = INT Qbar_11 z dz / INT Qbar_11 dz         (code line 70)
        D_eff = INT Qbar_11 (z - z_na)^2 dz                  (code line 72)

    (ii) 3-D EQUILIBRIUM gives the transverse shear from the axial-stress gradient:
            d sigma_13/dz = - d sigma_11/dx      (docstring line 10)
         Integrating from the traction-free bottom face:
            g(z) = INT_bot^z Qbar_11(zeta - z_na) dzeta      (code line 73, cumsum)
            sigma_13(z) = (g(z)/D_eff) V                     V = transverse shear force
         g(z) is the classical parabolic-type shear-flow shape; it is ZERO at both
         free surfaces (z=bot and z=top) by construction, satisfying the traction BCs.

    (iii) COMPLEMENTARY-ENERGY EQUIVALENCE fixes the stiffness so the shell shear
          strain energy equals the recovered 3-D shear energy:
            1/F = (1/D_eff^2) INT g(z)^2 / G_a(z) dz
            F   = D_eff^2 / INT g^2 / G_a dz                 (code line 74)
          This is Whitney's (1973) shear-correction, layup-dependent (g depends on
          the stack), with NO ad-hoc factor.  ISOTROPIC CHECK: a homogeneous plate
          gives F = (5/6) G h exactly (docstring line 19).

    (iv) COUPLED 2x2 (the default "MSG form", code lines 79-84):
            ghat_a = g_a / D_eff_a       (normalised shear flow, a = x,y)
            M_ab   = INT ghat_a ghat_b [Gbar^{-1}(z)]_ab dz  (lines 82-83)
            G_2x2  = M^{-1}                                   (line 84)
         Gbar(z) is the FULL rotated 2x2 ply transverse-shear matrix (line 34-36:
         Gbar = R2 Gp R2^T, Gp = diag(G13,G23)).  Off-axis plies make Gbar
         non-diagonal, coupling 13 and 23; M inverts the full complementary-shear
         flexibility, giving the coupling-aware 2x2.  When Gbar is diagonal (G13=G23
         or on-axis plies) M is diagonal and G reduces to the per-direction Whitney
         diagonal diag(Fx,Fy) (line 86, the coupled=False branch) -- the two agree.

RELATION TO YU (2005) -- BE PRECISE (for the paper)
    Yu (2005) derives the RM plate G by recasting the SECOND-ORDER asymptotically-
    correct plate energy into Reissner-Mindlin form and minimising the residual
    (principle of minimum information loss / SDP), NOT by a shear-flow quadrature.
    The construction HERE is the equilibrium/complementary-energy shear-flow method
    (Whitney 1973 lineage), which is the standard, defensible, correction-factor-FREE
    route and is asymptotically consistent for the transverse shear to leading order.
    THEREFORE: present G as "computed from the complementary energy of the layerwise
    shear flow (Whitney 1973), a coupling-aware generalisation, in the spirit of the
    asymptotically-correct, correction-factor-free construction of Yu (2005)."
    DO NOT claim it IS the Yu-2005 minimum-information-loss G unless proven equal.
    [This wording is already used in the paper theory_body.tex sec:wall.]

OUTPUT (line 91)
    returns (Gmat, recover, aux):
      Gmat = 2x2 transverse-shear stiffness (used in K_8x8 and the beam GA2/GA3).
      recover(z, V) -> [sigma13, sigma23] = [gx(z)/Dx*V[0], gy(z)/Dy*V[1]]  (lines 88-90)
        THE SAME shear-flow shapes g/D_eff that built G recover the transverse-shear
        STRESS from a transverse shear force V -- this is what the dehom uses (PART B).
      aux = (z-samples, ghat_x = gx/Dx, ghat_y = gy/Dy).
