--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION  --  RM is BETTER than KL for h/R = 0.12
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Classical (Euler-Bernoulli) terms -- extension EA, torsion GJ, bending
   EI2/EI3, and the extension-twist coupling C14 -- are matched by BOTH shell
   models to < 1 % (0.0 - 0.7 %). At zeroth order Kirchhoff-Love and
   Reissner-Mindlin coincide, so they agree with the solid and with each other.

2. Transverse-shear terms -- GA2, GA3, and the shear-bending couplings C25, C36
   -- are where the models separate:
        JAX-RM :  <= 0.63 %   (reproduces the solid)
        JAX-KL :  3.3 - 3.7 % low  (with exact curvature k22 = -1/R;
                                    with k22 = 0 the KL shear error grows to ~11 %)
   Kirchhoff-Love has NO transverse-shear degree of freedom; it recovers GA only
   through a second-order warping condensation that underpredicts the shear
   stiffness of a moderately thick (h/R = 0.12) wall and is sensitive to the
   curvature treatment. Reissner-Mindlin carries transverse shear directly and
   is both accurate and robust (curvature-insensitive: 0.5 % with k22 = 0 too).

3. The earlier GA2 != GA3 asymmetry (~13 % at the coarse N = 200 mesh) was a
   NODE-ON-AXIS DISCRETIZATION ARTIFACT, not model physics: a mesh-rotation test
   showed the KL split move with the mesh while the solid and RM stayed
   invariant. Refining to N = 3200 removes it (split -0.05 %). The residual
   3.6 % KL shear error is therefore genuine Kirchhoff physics, not mesh error.

4. BOTTOM LINE: for the h/R = 0.12 tube, JAX-RM reproduces the full 2D-solid
   Timoshenko 6x6 to < 1 % on EVERY term, whereas JAX-KL is accurate only on the
   classical terms and underpredicts transverse shear by 3.6 % (k22 = -1/R) to
   11 % (k22 = 0). RM is the better shell model at this thickness.
